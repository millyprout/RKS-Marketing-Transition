RKS Design Human-Centered Product Design & Innovation

RKS speaks as an experienced product design and innovation consultancy. Copy should be practical, credible, clear, client-focused, and grounded in real design work, business needs, and measurable outcomes.

Review: positioning, audience, tone, writing style, messaging hierarchy, strong examples, and common mistakes.

PA-AI The Human Intelligence Layer

PA-AI speaks as an educational and strategic thought leader. Copy should make complex ideas understandable, connect technology to human behavior, and establish Human Intelligence as essential to better AI decisions.

Review: positioning, Psycho-Aesthetics®, educational-first writing, founder voice, enterprise audience, strong examples, and common mistakes.

Brand review question

Before approving AI-generated copy, ask: Could this copy belong to either brand? If the answer is yes, it probably needs more specific positioning, language, evidence, and context.

AI as a Marketing Team Member

Shift from thinking of AI as a copy generator to treating it as a system of specialized collaborators that still require direction, context, review, and accountability.

AI is useful for Speed and structure

Research organization, brainstorming, outlines, first drafts, repurposing, documentation, variations, and repetitive formatting.

AI struggles with Judgment and truth

Current facts, nuanced brand decisions, stakeholder context, original insight, legal claims, emotional sensitivity, and knowing when it is wrong.

Human responsibility Direction and approval

Define the objective, supply reliable source material, select the strongest ideas, verify facts, refine the voice, and approve the final output.

System mindset Build repeatable workflows

Create reusable instructions, source libraries, prompt frameworks, and review steps instead of starting from a blank prompt every time.

Prompt Engineering

Strong outputs begin with a useful brief. Teach AI the same way you would brief a new team member.

01 Context

Explain the brand, project, goal, source material, and why the work matters.

02 Audience

Define who will read, watch, or use the output and what they should understand or do.

03 Task

State the exact deliverable, channel, format, length, and desired outcome.

04 Constraints

Provide voice rules, claims to avoid, required facts, examples, and formatting requirements.

05 Review & iterate

Give specific feedback, compare versions, verify facts, and refine until it is publish-ready.

Building Custom GPTs

A custom GPT turns repeated context, rules, examples, and source files into a reusable marketing assistant. It should reduce setup time without removing human review.

Single task

Prompt

A specific instruction used for one output or one step. Best for a clearly defined request that does not require a permanent knowledge base.

Ongoing work

Project

A workspace that keeps related conversations, files, and context together for an initiative, campaign, or area of work.

Reusable specialist

Custom GPT

A configured assistant with standing instructions, rules, knowledge files, conversation starters, and repeatable behavior.

AI Quality Control

AI output is always a draft until a person verifies the facts, improves the thinking, applies the brand voice, and confirms that the content is appropriate to publish.

Verify every factual claim

Check names, dates, statistics, product details, business claims, links, and current information against reliable original sources.

Never invent quotes

Use only approved transcripts, articles, interviews, or direct source material. Confirm wording and attribution before publishing.

Protect the brand voice

Remove generic AI language, unnecessary hype, repetition, vague claims, awkward transitions, and phrasing that could belong to any company.

Review copyright and ownership

Do not assume generated or found text, images, music, footage, or concepts are automatically cleared for commercial use.

Avoid SEO spam

Write for people first. Do not force keywords, repeat phrases unnaturally, overproduce thin content, or publish unhelpful AI summaries.

Keep sensitive information out

Do not upload confidential client information, passwords, private personnel details, unreleased work, or restricted files without approval.

Watch for false confidence

AI can present incorrect information persuasively. Treat confident wording as a reason to verify, not as evidence that the answer is accurate.

Make the final judgment yourself

Helena remains responsible for whether the content is useful, accurate, on-brand, appropriately approved, and ready to publish.
