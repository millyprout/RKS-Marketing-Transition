Approval Principles

The review path changes by brand, asset type, message risk, and publishing destination.

  • Start with the initial point of contact for the brand or workstream.
  • Do not send unfinished work directly to leadership unless explicitly requested.
  • Use specialist reviewers only for the area they own: visual, SEO, website, video, PR, or technical accuracy.
  • Clearly state what decision is needed and when feedback is required.
  • Helena owns consolidating feedback, implementing revisions, and moving the work to publication.

Requesting Approval

Use this six-step sequence as the standard operating process for marketing work.

01Reach out to the initial POC

Confirm the objective, audience, channel, source material, timing, and correct review route.

02Refine the material

Develop the asset and resolve basic content, visual, or technical issues before leadership review.

03Send to the leadership POC

Share a clear, review-ready version with the appropriate RKS or PA-AI leadership contact.

04Gather feedback

Collect comments in one place and clarify any conflicting direction.

05Implement

Apply approved changes, complete quality control, and prepare the final asset.

06Publish, post, or schedule

Release as needed, store the final version, and update the project status.

Every review request should include

What the asset is, the intended audience, where it will appear, the decision needed, the working-file link, and the feedback deadline.
